The big story today: Red across the board. The watchlist bled broadly on Friday's session, with 20 tickers flashing big-move alerts and 11 showing unusual volume spikes. The most likely culprit: a risk-off rotation as markets digest the latest Fed commentary and rising Treasury yields.

What Stood Out in the Deep Dives

SELLAS Life Sciences (SLS, score: 72) remains the most compelling near-term catalyst play on the entire watchlist. Their REGAL Phase 3 trial has hit 72 of 80 required events — the final readout could literally drop any day. They're sitting on $114M in cash (4+ years of runway), so this isn't a "running out of money" story. The stock pulled back 8.5% on Friday, which could be a gift if the data reads positive. But make no mistake: this is binary. Failure here craters the stock.

PolyPid (PYPD, score: 62) is quietly approaching a huge inflection: their rolling NDA submission is due by end of March — meaning this week or next. A partnership announcement could come alongside it. This one has been under the radar but could move fast.

AST SpaceMobile (ASTS, score: 70) has their BlueBird 7 satellite launch on Blue Origin's New Glenn imminent, with a cadence of one launch every 45 days planned throughout 2026. The space-based cellular network thesis is either visionary or a pipe dream, but the near-term news flow is undeniable.

Danger Zone

Ayr Wellness (AYRWF, score: 12) scored the lowest on the entire watchlist at 12 — the deep dive flagged serious going-concern risk, heavy debt load, and no clear path to profitability. Down 12.5% on Friday. This is the clearest "avoid" signal in the data.

Bloom Energy (BE, score: 57) dropped nearly 10% — the largest dollar-value decline on the board at -$16.57. Still has decent fundamentals but the selloff suggests the market is re-pricing the clean energy space.

Uranium Bright Spot

Ur-Energy (URG, score: 51) was the lone standout gainer, up nearly 10% against the tide. Their Shirley Basin mine startup is imminent (within weeks), which would generate $30M+ in annual free cash flow. This is a real catalyst, not just hype.

The Week Ahead

Multiple earnings reports land March 31: Planet Labs (PLNH), Bitfarms (BITF), and Nano Dimension (NNDM) all report Q4 2025. Each is at an inflection point — PLNH needs to show the California exit is clean, BITF needs to prove the HPC/AI pivot is real, and NNDM's strategic alternatives update could be market-moving.

Bottom line: Despite the broad red day, the catalyst calendar is stacked. SLS's REGAL readout, PYPD's NDA deadline, and three Q4 earnings all land in the next 8 days. This is a week to watch closely, not panic. The deep dives show most positions still have solid fundamentals — this pullback is more macro than micro.
