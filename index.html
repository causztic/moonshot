// What’s Hot Today

Coverage note: Partial publish. Missing or invalid batch output for Clean Energy, Battery & Uranium.

The tape is opening with materially weaker breadth than the April 8 board. Yesterday's squeeze-heavy tone has cooled into a selective risk market: only 15 names are up against 50 down, so the morning read is less about chasing momentum and more about figuring out which stories still have sponsorship when the speculative bid fades.

Biotech is still the steadiest pocket, but it is not a clean risk-on signal. VNDA is leading at +6.9% and names like CRSP, BEAM, NTLA, PBYI, and TARA are green, which keeps healthcare catalysts relevant; at the same time TSHA is giving back ground and cash-sensitive names like CRBU and CING are getting hit, so leadership is staying narrow even inside the sector.

Outside biotech, the fragile narratives are mostly being sold. Quantum is red across the board with QBTS, IONQ, RGTI, and QUBT all lower; eVTOL is 0-for-4 with EVTL down 5.6% and JOBY and ACHR both off more than 3%; AI is mostly weaker in SYM, SERV, FIVN, UPST, and BBAI. The few exceptions, mainly SIDU in space data and ONDS in space wireless, look like isolated pockets rather than the start of a broad rotation.

Balance-sheet stress is where the tape looks most punitive. The under-six-month runway group is full of names already trading badly: ARBK is effectively at zero months, AYRWF, CRBU, and PYPD sit near two months, PLNH and SLDP around four, and AI and JOBY around five. Clean energy, cannabis, and crypto mining are where that pressure is showing up most obviously, and UEC is missing from the morning sheet, but the rest of uranium is weak enough that the sector read does not change.

Heavy insider selling is not helping the higher-beta stories. Recent sale clusters show up across JOBY and ACHR in eVTOL, QBTS, RGTI, and QUBT in quantum, SYM and SERV in AI automation and robotics, SEZL in fintech, STEM in clean energy, and CRBU and TSHA in biotech; when that ownership pattern meets weak price action, it usually means the market needs a much better catalyst before it will pay up for the narrative again.

The next real test is the earnings calendar. JKS on April 16 and QS on April 22 start the next catalyst cluster, then April 29 through mid-May brings a much denser run across clean energy, AI, biotech, quantum, space, and eVTOL. Bottom line: this looks like a proof market, not a hope market. Names with visible catalysts and enough cash can still hold up, but the fragile stories are trading like financing risks first and moonshots second.
